NCat 29 Mar 2022 05:02

I don't believe this forum is the greatest place to go to for in-game playing advice, since this forum is mostly dedicated to the running and maintanace of the 5e wikidot itself. Might I recommend some places such as r/dndnext and r/dnd on reddit, or alternatively the GiTP Forums if you prefer a non reddit website?

As for your issue itself, it might not fall under the usual expertises of 'rules lawyers' or 'contract specialists', since the issue itself finds itself to be not very related to the rules of the game itself.

My personal recommendation is just, dont deal with devils? It never goes well in game, because most stories are designed to have that always play out poorly, so unless you getting a bad deal with a fiend is part of the story you want to write?

cottoneyenoh 29 Mar 2022 14:43

yea i'm in agreement with ncat, this isn't exactly the place for questions like these since we're focused on providing players and dms with the mechanical tools for playing 5e rather than helping with in-game issues. other forums are better suited for offering in-game advice, and i'd recommend you ask around there if you need further assistance.

(additionally, if i'm understanding your question right, it doesn't seem like there's much of a contract to make? like digging through your question, it sounds like your tiefling/centaur-tiefling/whatever party member needed a disguise, and their patron turned them into a jackalope and said it would be permanent if you/your party didn't get the soul of the country's regent (i'm not gonna touch on the whole bunny girl thing, that's between you and your fellow players). there aren't exactly any benefits i can tell that are directed at you that would entail a contract or anything, and considering the demon is the tiefling's patron, it seems like it doesn't involve you aside from them being in your party. consequentially, because your question neither relates to the rules of the game nor an actual contract, this is probably a problem that you need to solve in-session yourself.)
