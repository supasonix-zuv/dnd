Re: Mordenkainen's Monsters of the Multiverse
cottoneyenoh 07 Feb 2022 00:35

MMOM really does throw a wrench in our whole organization of these pages. one solution may be to make new pages for the MMOM races, but if we don't clarify the difference between the long-standing 5e races and the MMOM races, it could get really confusing really quickly. maybe making a new section above the races that has the MMOM versions and marking the current race section as something else (it really does suck because I don't want to say "archive the old races" or "declare they're outdated" since 1) not all of the races were updated, and even if they were, 2) it's not like the old races were suddenly made irrelevant by the release of MMOM, especially since it's only available in that overpriced bundle for the next 3 months).

i also want to make sure that whatever we do, it's not going to make things harder as WOTC releases new content (i was going to suggest making pages for the new races just titled "MMOM-elf" or whatever, but if every future book follows the same format or they make updates for other races, then we're shooting ourselves in the foot by labelling everything as "new"). a solution that will take a lot of work but could be the best of all worlds is making pages for each race from each publication and grouping them as such (dmg-elf, e:rflw-warforged, wbw-fairy, UA78-kobald, etc). the problem is that this could easily bloat the races page, especially given the tendency to release subraces in UA or some setting-specific books (just look at the sheer amount of elves we'd have to make pages for, plus how would we handle subraces like the lotusden halfling? put all of the info for halflings on that page but add the subrace details too? that's a lot of redundant text when you're covering every single race)

in summary, i have no idea how we should handle this and i'd love feedback from y'all, and while i like where WOTC is going by rebalancing (sub)races and stuff, they really made this tricky and frustrating for anyone trying to catalogue 5e content for public use, especially when you don't want to throw out everything that's not the most cutting-edge content. it's the reason why i really like our archived UA pages: it preserves earlier versions of subclasses that people may have found interesting. for example, we make the mystic class available and don't just throw it away just because we ended up getting the psi warrior and aberrant soul subclasses out of it, and i like that we have the psychic warrior and the psi knight even though the psi warrior ended up being what was released

in any case, the main page or individual race pages are probably going to get a lot more confusing in the coming future

fredbob, the lineage pages look perfect (especially the general page at http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/lineage once we get the MMOM versions put up), i guess the only question is how we'll format it on the main page. as amazing as that general page is, it's a bit bulky to transfer to the main page in its entirety unless we only include the links to the "lineage:___" pages. in any case, i didn't know you were working on that already and having that sort of masterpost really makes the whole process easier (even if we need to make new pages for stuff like duregar and shadar-kai and whatever)
